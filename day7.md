## 🧠 What I Learned Today

Today I built a **personalized Claude usage strategy** based on my actual profile:

- 🎓 Student
- 💻 Activities: Coding, Research & Learning, Marketing & Business Planning
- 📅 Frequency: Daily user
- 🎯 Primary output need: Business Strategy & Creative Work

---

## 🤖 The Three Models — When to Use Each

### ⚡ Haiku — Speed & Simplicity
Use when you need a fast answer, not a deep one.

**Best for:**
- Quick definitions or factual lookups
- Rapid brainstorming (10 ideas in 30 seconds)
- Summarizing short texts
- Simple code completions

**Don't use for:** Complex reasoning, strategy, multi-step coding problems.

---

### 🔵 Sonnet — Your Daily Workhorse ⭐ PRIMARY
Use for 70%+ of your daily tasks. Smart, fast, and high quality.

**Best for:**
- Debugging and writing code
- Research summaries
- Marketing copy and content
- Academic essays and reports
- Learning complex topics
- Most business writing

**Why it works:** It's the ideal balance between intelligence, speed, and output quality for a student doing varied, daily work.

---

### 💎 Opus — Strategic Depth
Reserve for high-stakes, complex, multi-angle work.

**Best for:**
- Full business plans and go-to-market strategies
- Competitive market analysis
- Investor pitch content
- Major capstone projects or final deliverables
- Problems that require deep, structured reasoning

**Warning:** Don't use Opus for everything — it's a premium resource. Use it intentionally.

---

## ⚡ Effort Levels Explained

Think of effort as how long Claude "thinks" before responding. Higher effort = deeper reasoning, but slower and costs more quota.

| Effort | When to Use | Example Tasks |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| 🟢 Low | Fast, light tasks | Quick Q&A, brainstorming lists, simple lookups |
| 🟡 Standard | Everyday default | Code writing, essays, marketing copy, summaries |
| 🟠 High | Deeper reasoning needed | Business strategy, detailed research, architecture decisions |
| 🔴 Max | Final high-stakes deliverables | Investor pitches, full business plans, capstone submissions |

**My default:** Standard effort for 75% of tasks. High for research/strategy. Max only for final deliverables.

## 🔄 My Optimal Daily Workflow

```
🌅 Morning     → Haiku + Low       → Quick catch-up, definitions, fast brainstorms
💻 Work Hours  → Sonnet + Standard → Code, essays, content, summaries
🔬 Deep Work   → Sonnet + High     → Research, complex problems, analysis
🚀 High-Stakes → Opus + High/Max   → Business plans, pitches, final deliverables
```

---

## 🚫 Biggest Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using Opus for everything** — It's powerful but wastes quota. Sonnet covers 70%+ of daily needs.
2. **Using Max effort for routine tasks** — Standard effort handles most work just fine.
3. **Never using Haiku** — For quick lookups and brainstorming, Haiku is faster and lighter.
4. **Vague prompts with high effort** — More effort ≠ better results if your prompt is vague. Be specific first.
5. **Skipping context** — Always tell Claude your role, goal, and constraints. It dramatically improves output quality.

## 🏆 Final Recommendation

> **If I could use only ONE model and ONE effort level for most of my work:**
>
> ### Claude Sonnet + Standard Effort
>
> For a student who codes, researches, and strategizes daily — Sonnet at Standard effort covers 80% of real-world needs with exceptional quality and speed. It's smart enough for complex reasoning, fast enough for daily use, and efficient enough to preserve quota for moments when Opus is truly needed.

---

## 💡 Golden Rule

> **Better prompts beat better models.**
>
> Before upgrading to Opus or Max effort, ask yourself: *"Did I give Claude enough context, constraints, and a clear goal?"*
>
> A great prompt on Sonnet Standard will outperform a vague prompt on Opus Max — every time.
